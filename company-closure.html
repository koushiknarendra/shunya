what is company strike off?

the legal process to permanently close a company that has stopped operations.

STK

Strike Off (STK-2)

faster & more affordable

For companies with no active business, no outstanding loans, and no pending litigations. The company applies via Form STK-2 and the ROC removes it from the register. Typically 3–6 months.

WU

Winding Up

for complex cases

For companies with outstanding liabilities, active assets to distribute, or ongoing disputes. Requires tribunal or court involvement. We assess which route applies and advise before you proceed.

common questions

How long does company closure take?

For a voluntary strike off via Form STK-2, the typical timeline is 3–6 months from filing to the ROC's gazette notification. The timeline depends on pending annual filings, outstanding dues, and MCA processing time.

Can we close the company if we have pending GST or TDS dues?

Pending dues must be cleared before filing for strike off. Our CA will assess your compliance status and help you clear overdue filings before initiating the STK-2 process. This is included in the ₹9,999 service.

What does ₹9,999 include?

The fee covers: STK-2 filing, CA-certified declarations (DIR-12, GNL-2, indemnity bond as applicable), clearance of up to one year of pending annual returns, MCA21 filing fees, and complete ROC correspondence until closure. No hidden charges.

What if we have overdue annual returns?

Companies with default in annual filings can still apply for strike off. Our CA will file the outstanding returns and regularise the company before submitting the STK-2 application. This is part of the service.
