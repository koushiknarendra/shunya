common questions

What exactly is CCFS-2026 and why does it matter now?

CCFS-2026 (Companies Compliance Facilitation Scheme, 2026) is a one-time scheme issued by the Ministry of Corporate Affairs via Circular 01/2026. It reduces the STK-2 government filing fee from ₹10,000 to ₹2,500 — a saving of ₹7,500 on the government fee alone. If you have an inactive company and were considering closure, now is the cheapest time to do it. The scheme expires on 15th July 2026.

Is the July 15 deadline firm? What happens after that?

July 15, 2026 is the last date to file Form STK-2 under the CCFS-2026 concessional fee structure. After that, the standard government fee of ₹10,000 applies. Your company can still be closed after July 15 — but you will pay ₹7,500 more on the government fee alone. If you're going to do this, doing it before the deadline simply makes financial sense.

Can I apply under CCFS-2026 if my company has missed annual filings?

Yes. CCFS-2026 also covers regularisation of pending filings — you can clear overdue annual returns as part of this scheme without attracting additional penalties beyond the concessional fee. Our CA will assess exactly which filings are pending and what they will cost before we begin. Nothing happens without your approval.

Why is there a cost range? Can I get an exact number?

The government STK-2 fee is fixed at ₹2,500 under CCFS-2026. The variable portion depends on how many years of returns are pending and how complex the case is. Once you share your company details, our CA reviews the MCA portal and gives you the exact total — not a range. This assessment is completely free and no commitment is required.

Do I pay anything before seeing the cost estimate?

No. You share your company name and details. A CA reviews the case and sends you the exact cost breakdown within 4 hours. You decide whether to proceed. Payment only happens after you've seen and approved the quote. There is no consultation fee and no commitment from filling this form.

How long does company closure take?

Under the STK-2 (strike off) route, the typical timeline is 3–6 months from filing to the Gazette notification. This includes a mandatory 30-day public objection window after the ROC issues its notice. Filing under CCFS-2026 before July 15 does not change the timeline — it only reduces the government fee you pay.
