How to file Form 15CA online on the Income Tax portal?

Quick Answer

Log in to incometax.gov.in, go to e-File, then Income Tax Forms, then File Income Tax Forms, and search for Form 15CA. Select the correct Part based on your remittance amount and taxability. For Part C (above Rs 5 lakhs, taxable), you must have the CA-issued 15CB acknowledgement number before you can submit.

Full Answer

A freelance designer in Kochi filed Form 15CA Part A for a payment of Rs 8 lakhs to a US client for sub-contracted work. Part A is for exempt remittances below Rs 5 lakhs. His payment was above Rs 5 lakhs and was taxable as FTS. His bank rejected the 15CA as the wrong Part. He lost two days and had to start over, this time with a CA to issue 15CB and file Part C. The Part selection is where most first-time filers go wrong.

Before starting the filing, determine which Part applies to your specific remittance. This is the most important decision in the process and the one most people do not spend enough time on.

Log in to incometax.gov.in using your PAN and password. If you do not have a registered account, you can register using your PAN, Aadhaar-linked mobile number, and email address. Verification through Aadhaar OTP takes a few minutes.

From your dashboard, go to e-File, select Income Tax Forms, then File Income Tax Forms. In the search field, type 15CA. The form will appear. Click File Now.

Part A applies when your remittance is below Rs 5 lakhs in the financial year and the payment is not chargeable to tax in India. This includes most personal remittances that fall within the Rule 37BB exempt categories but which your bank has asked you to document anyway.

Part B applies when your remittance is below Rs 5 lakhs and is chargeable to tax. You assess the taxability yourself, no CA certificate is required. This Part is used less frequently because most taxable business payments exceed Rs 5 lakhs.

Part C applies when your remittance is above Rs 5 lakhs and is chargeable to tax. This is the most common Part for business payments. You cannot complete Part C without a valid 15CB acknowledgement number from a CA. The CA must upload 15CB on the portal before you begin this filing.

Part D applies when the remittance is not chargeable to tax under any provision of the Income Tax Act or under a DTAA, regardless of amount. For example, payments that are genuinely business income of a foreign entity with no PE in India, certified as such by a CA.

Once you select the correct Part, fill in your details as the remitter: PAN, name, address, email, and the specific bank account from which the payment will go. Then fill in the foreign beneficiary details: full name, address, country, and their bank account information.

Enter the remittance amount, the foreign currency, the Indian rupee equivalent, and the purpose of remittance using the drop-down purpose code list. This purpose code must match the code you provide on your bank's A2 or remittance declaration form. Mismatched purpose codes cause downstream reconciliation issues.

For Part C, there is a specific field for the 15CB acknowledgement number. Enter it exactly as it appears on the 15CB certificate. The portal validates this number in real time.

Submit and download the acknowledgement PDF. This is the document your bank needs. Submit it along with the 15CB printout and the bank's own forex declaration form.

One timing rule under the Income Tax Rules: do not file 15CA more than seven days before the actual remittance date. The withdrawal window after filing is only seven days, and only if the bank has not yet processed the transfer. If the remittance is delayed beyond seven days after you file, you will need to withdraw and refile.

Note: Rates and thresholds mentioned are as of FY 2024-25. Always verify current figures before acting.

Frequently Asked Question

What happens if I select the wrong Part of Form 15CA and the bank rejects it?

You can withdraw a filed 15CA within seven days of filing, provided the bank has not yet processed the remittance. After withdrawal, file the correct Part. If Part C is the correct Part and you originally filed Part A or B, you will also need to obtain a 15CB from a CA before you can complete the new Part C filing.
