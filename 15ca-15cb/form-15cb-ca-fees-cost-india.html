How much does it cost to get Form 15CB from a CA?

Quick Answer

Form 15CB fees in India range from Rs 1,500 to Rs 5,000 for most standard remittances. DTAA benefit claims, ambiguous payment classification, and urgent turnarounds push fees higher. Online services typically offer transparent flat pricing. Choosing based on price alone without assessing the CA's foreign remittance experience creates compliance risk.

Full Answer

A product startup in Bengaluru found a CA on a freelance platform offering 15CB at Rs 600 per certificate. The CA delivered in two hours. The 15CB stated TDS was not applicable, citing the India-US DTAA, but the TDS section was left blank without any analysis. When the bank reviewed the certificate, they asked why the applicable DTAA article was not cited and why the beneficial ownership condition was not addressed. The bank's forex compliance officer escalated it internally. The startup needed a revised certificate from a different CA. What was Rs 600 became Rs 4,500 plus a four-day delay on a time-sensitive vendor payment.

Form 15CB is a signed legal certificate. The fee reflects the work required to produce a certificate that can withstand bank scrutiny and potential Income Tax department review. Buying the cheapest option is a false economy when the certificate itself carries legal weight.

For a straightforward remittance where the invoice is clear, the nature of payment is unambiguous (a pure services contract or a clear product purchase), no DTAA benefit is being claimed, and TDS has been correctly deducted and deposited, most practicing CAs charge between Rs 1,500 and Rs 2,500 outside metros. In Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, and Hyderabad, the same clean transaction typically costs Rs 2,500 to Rs 4,000.

What pushes fees higher is complexity. DTAA benefit analysis requires the CA to review the specific treaty, confirm the applicable article, verify the Tax Residency Certificate from the foreign entity, assess the beneficial ownership condition, and certify the reduced rate. This adds meaningful analysis time and typically adds Rs 1,000 to Rs 2,500 to the base fee.

Payment classification analysis adds cost when the nature of the payment is genuinely ambiguous. A contract that describes work as both technical consulting and software maintenance, for example, requires the CA to determine whether Section 9(1)(vi) (royalty) or Section 9(1)(vii) (FTS) applies. If there is relevant ITAT or High Court case law specific to the payment type, the CA needs to review it. This is not padding; it is the work that protects you from an incorrect certification.

Urgency carries a premium. A CA who reorganises their existing queue to deliver a certificate in three to four hours instead of the standard next-day timeline will charge 40 to 80 percent more for the express slot. This is reasonable. Express fees are common in professional services and the alternative is the payment being delayed by a day.

For NRI repatriation certificates where the scope extends to reviewing ITR compliance, income source documentation, and property transaction records, the 15CB is typically bundled into a broader NRI tax engagement. These engagements run from Rs 8,000 to Rs 25,000 or more depending on the complexity of the income sources and the number of years of ITR filings involved.

For businesses with recurring foreign payments, per-certificate pricing is inefficient. A monthly retainer covering a defined number of certificates, say five to ten, plus same-day turnaround for urgent requests, typically costs Rs 6,000 to Rs 20,000 per month depending on transaction volume and average complexity. This is significantly less per certificate than individual pricing and gives you priority turnaround without negotiating every time.

Under ICAI's guidelines, a CA issuing a 15CB certificate is responsible for verifying the facts and is professionally accountable for the certificate's contents. A certificate issued without proper verification is a disciplinary matter under the ICAI Code of Ethics. When evaluating a CA for 15CB work, ask specifically about their experience with foreign remittances, the typical range of payment types they handle, and whether they have dealt with DTAA analysis for the specific country involved in your transaction.

Frequently Asked Question

Can I negotiate the fee with a CA for recurring 15CB certificates on the same vendor every month?

Yes. For recurring payments to the same vendor where the CA has already done the initial classification analysis and DTAA review, subsequent certificates require less work. Most CAs reduce fees for repeat transactions involving the same payment type and foreign entity. A monthly retainer arrangement formalises this and gives you predictable pricing, priority service, and a CA already familiar with your vendor relationships.
